# Marketing Rules — The Emotional Ladder (internal model)

> Adapted from Dr. David Hawkins' Map of Consciousness. Used here purely as an
> internal targeting lens for *which emotion to enter at*. It is not science and
> never appears in customer-facing copy (see Rule 14).

## The model in one paragraph

For marketing, the map is an emotional-altitude scale. Every prospect has a
dominant emotional state about your category, and it sits somewhere on a ladder.
Below the Courage line (≈200) she's contracted, blaming, avoiding — shame, guilt,
apathy, grief, fear, desire, anger, pride. At or above it she's agentic — courage,
neutrality, willingness, acceptance, reason. **Buying happens above the line.**
The entire job of a funnel is to walk her across it, one rung at a time.

## The rung → marketing read (internal only)

- **Shame / Guilt** — "something's wrong with me / I'm doing it wrong." Frozen. Unsellable until reframed.
- **Apathy** — "nothing works, why bother." Scrolls past everything. The pit you must never push her into.
- **Grief** — "I miss who I was." Open to belonging and being understood.
- **Fear** — "what's happening, is this permanent." Attention-grabbing, fragile, can collapse to apathy.
- **Desire** — "I want that outcome." The buying engine.
- **Anger** — "it's not fair, no one warned me, I was dismissed." High energy, action-ready if pointed.
- **Pride** — "I've handled everything else, I don't need help." Defensive wall. Use, don't attack.
- **Courage (the line)** — "I can take one step." This is where the first micro-action lives.
- **Neutrality / Acceptance** — "okay, here's where I stand, it's workable." Results and diagnosis land here.
- **Willingness / Reason** — "show me the path, I'm ready." Offer, plan, evidence, close.

## The rules

**1. Enter at her rung, not yours.** Brands default to broadcasting from where they sit — Reason, Authority, mission. She's usually three rungs lower, in Grief or Fear or Anger. The hook must sound like her current internal monologue in the first five words, or she's gone.

**2. Move her up exactly one rung per asset.** Emotions climb in steps. You cannot pull someone from Apathy to Reason in a single ad — the jump isn't believable and reads as "not for me." One asset lifts one rung. The full climb happens across the funnel, not inside any one piece.

**3. Everything points at the Courage step.** The most important thing in the funnel is the first tiny, agentic action that crosses the line — the quiz click, the free score, the "check your X." It must be low-risk and high-agency. Below-the-line emotions convert into action only when handed an immediate easy step. Design that step first, then write backward to the emotion.

**4. Map each funnel stage to a rung and let the stage do the lifting.** Ad enters at her emotion (often below the line). First micro-action = Courage. Results/diagnosis = Neutrality/Acceptance. Offer/plan = Willingness/Reason. Each stage should assume she arrived one rung higher than the last left her.

**5. Match altitude to traffic temperature.** Cold traffic enters low — problem, fear, grief, curiosity — because she's raw and in the symptom. Warm and retargeting traffic enters higher — status, authority, identity — because she's already moved up. High messaging on cold traffic talks over her head; low messaging on warm traffic re-traumatizes and insults.

**6. Fear and anger sell, but only with the exit bolted on.** Negative states grab attention and can collapse downward into Apathy ("it's hopeless," "they're all scammers"). Every negative-emotion hook must close on the agency step in the same breath. If a line ends on the pain instead of the next step, cut it. Fear without a door is just despair, and despair scrolls.

**7. Reframe to lift her off shame and guilt.** Self-blame freezes action. The "it's not your fault, it's [external mechanism]" reframe is the lever that lifts her from Guilt toward action rungs and the line. Deploy it early in problem-led copy.

**8. Desire is the buying rung — use it, don't camp on it.** Wanting the outcome is the engine of conversion, but pure desire-stacking ("you'll be hotter/richer/happier") reads as hype and trips the skeptic's defenses. Anchor desire in a believable mechanism and an easy first step.

**9. Pride is a wall, not a win.** The high-functioning prospect is above most negatives but still below the line and defensive. Don't attack the wall. Position the action as what smart, proactive people already do, so taking the step *confirms* her identity instead of admitting failure.

**10. Authority and reason are closers, not openers.** Evidence, credentials, and mechanism reassure someone who's already emotionally in. They do nothing for someone in raw fear or grief — she can't process logic from a contracted state. Lead with emotion, validate with authority, close with reason.

**11. Don't run mission or belonging copy to cold traffic.** Brand-purpose and transcendence messaging sits at the top of the ladder. It resonates with loyal, warm audiences and converts almost nobody cold. Mission is a retention and community lever, not an acquisition hook.

**12. Keep the rung constant across every handoff.** The ad's emotional promise must equal what the next screen delivers. If the ad enters at grief-and-belonging and the landing page opens with clinical authority, the altitude jump breaks trust and she drops. Click to next screen must feel like the same conversation.

**13. Read comments to locate her actual rung.** The audience's own language tells you where they sit. Blame = Guilt/Shame. "It's not fair / I was dismissed" = Anger. "I feel lost / I miss who I was" = Grief. "Is this even real?" = skeptical Pride/Reason. Write the next ad to enter at the most common rung you see.

**14. Never put the framework in front of the customer.** No "consciousness," "levels," "vibration," "frequency," "raise your vibration." It's pseudoscience, it repels, and for evidence-minded audiences it nukes credibility on contact. The ladder is an internal targeting tool. Customer-facing copy uses plain human emotion words only.

**15. Audit every asset with two questions.** (a) What rung does this enter at, and is that actually where the audience is? (b) Does it end by handing her the single next step, one rung up? If either answer is no, it isn't ready to run.
